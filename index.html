Notes:
  • This is an example of a very simple insurance product which involves no unit-linked funds or customised payment frequency.
  • This is based on the equivalence principle where the benefits paid is matched to the premiums required, solely dependent on the risk of death and an assumed risk-free rate of 4% per annum.
  • This is a net premium calculator. Hence, it does not take into account the expenses of an insurance company: management fee, commission, transfer fee, etc.
  • It is assumed that smoking increases mortality risk by 30%.
  • The life table used is taken from the Global Health Observatory data repository. Link: https://apps.who.int/gho/data/view.main.60750?lang=en
  • The assumptions used will be different the those used in real life. Different companies will have different assumptions.
  • In practice, there will likely be a more complex underwriting process before the premiums can be calculated.
